Lupus Care in Lagos

Lupus is a complex autoimmune condition that can affect different parts of the body. Early recognition, careful diagnosis, and ongoing specialist care are important in helping patients manage symptoms and monitor disease activity.

What is lupus?

Lupus is an autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks the body’s own tissues. It may affect the joints, skin, kidneys, blood cells, and other organs, and symptoms can vary widely from person to person.

Common symptoms

  • Joint pain or swelling
  • Fatigue
  • Skin rashes
  • Fever or unexplained illness
  • Symptoms that come and go over time

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosing lupus usually involves a detailed clinical review because symptoms may overlap with other conditions. Assessment may include medical history, examination, and review of relevant tests where necessary.

Treatment approach

Treatment focuses on controlling disease activity, reducing inflammation, protecting organ health, monitoring flare-ups, and supporting long-term wellbeing. Care plans depend on the organs involved, severity, and the patient’s health needs.

When to book

Book an appointment if you have symptoms suggestive of autoimmune disease, a previous lupus diagnosis, or recurrent joint pain and systemic symptoms that need specialist evaluation.
