Lupus Care in Lagos

Lupus is a complex autoimmune condition that can affect different parts of the body, including the joints, skin, blood, kidneys, and other organs. Early recognition, careful diagnosis, and ongoing specialist care can help patients better manage symptoms, monitor disease activity, and plan long-term treatment.

What is lupus?

Lupus is an autoimmune disease in which the immune system mistakenly attacks the body’s own tissues. Because it can affect different organs and body systems, symptoms may vary widely from one person to another. Some patients experience mainly joint and skin symptoms, while others may need closer monitoring for more complex disease activity.

Common symptoms

Joint pain or swelling

Lupus may cause aching, swelling, or stiffness in the joints, sometimes with flare-ups.

Fatigue

Many people with lupus experience persistent tiredness that affects daily activities.

Skin rashes

Rashes may occur, especially in people whose lupus affects the skin.

Fever or unexplained illness

Some patients report episodes of feeling unwell, with symptoms that come and go.

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosing lupus usually involves a detailed clinical assessment because symptoms may overlap with other conditions. This may include medical history, physical examination, and review of relevant tests when needed. A specialist approach is important because lupus can present in different ways and may require long-term monitoring.

Treatment approach

Treatment focuses on controlling disease activity, reducing inflammation, protecting organ health, monitoring flare-ups, and supporting long-term wellbeing. Care plans depend on the organs involved, the severity of symptoms, and the individual patient’s overall health needs.

When to book

You should consider booking an appointment if you have symptoms suggestive of autoimmune disease, a previous lupus diagnosis, recurrent joint pain, unexplained fatigue, skin symptoms, or other ongoing concerns that need specialist evaluation and follow-up.
