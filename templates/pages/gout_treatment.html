What is gout?

Gout is a form of inflammatory arthritis linked to uric acid crystal deposits in the joints. It often affects one joint at a time, especially the big toe, but may also affect the ankles, knees, feet, wrists, elbows, or other joints. Some patients experience isolated flare-ups, while others may need ongoing care for recurrent disease.

Common symptoms

Sudden severe joint pain

Pain may begin suddenly and can be intense, especially during an acute flare.

Redness and swelling

The affected joint may become swollen, red, and visibly inflamed.

Warmth and tenderness

The joint may feel hot, tender, and painful even to light touch.

Repeated attacks over time

Some people experience recurrent flare-ups that require long-term prevention planning.

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosis is based on the pattern of symptoms, medical history, examination, and where necessary, supporting investigations. Specialist review helps separate gout from other causes of joint pain, swelling, and inflammatory arthritis, so treatment can be better tailored to the patient’s needs.

Treatment approach

Treatment may involve relief of acute flare-ups, prevention of future attacks, identification of contributing factors, and long-term strategies for patients with recurrent gout or more complicated disease patterns. The goal is to reduce pain, control inflammation, and help prevent repeat episodes.

When to book

You should consider booking an appointment if you have sudden attacks of severe joint pain, repeated swelling episodes, pain that keeps returning, or if you have previously been told you have gout and need specialist review or follow-up care.
