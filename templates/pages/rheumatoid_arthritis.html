What is rheumatoid arthritis?

Rheumatoid arthritis is a chronic inflammatory disease in which the immune system mistakenly attacks the joints. It commonly affects the hands, wrists, feet, and other joints, and may interfere with movement, comfort, grip strength, and daily activities. In some patients, symptoms can extend beyond the joints, which is why careful specialist review is important.

Common symptoms

Morning stiffness

Stiffness that lasts for a prolonged period after waking can be an important clue.

Swollen or painful joints

Joints may feel warm, tender, swollen, or painful, especially during flare-ups.

Pain in multiple joints

Symptoms often affect more than one joint and may involve the hands, wrists, feet, or knees.

Fatigue and reduced energy

Some people feel unusually tired or limited in daily activity because of inflammation.

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosis depends on a detailed clinical assessment. This usually includes a careful review of symptoms, physical examination of the joints, and consideration of prior medical history and investigations where relevant. Early diagnosis is valuable because timely treatment can help reduce inflammation and protect joint function.

Treatment approach

Treatment plans are tailored to the individual and may include patient education, medicines to control inflammation, symptom relief, disease monitoring, and ongoing follow-up. The aim is to improve comfort, reduce disease activity, preserve joint function, and support quality of life over time.

When to book

You should consider booking an appointment if you have persistent joint swelling, prolonged morning stiffness, pain affecting multiple joints, or if you already have a rheumatoid arthritis diagnosis and need specialist review, monitoring, or follow-up care.
