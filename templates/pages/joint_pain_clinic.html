What this clinic page covers

This page is for people with persistent joint pain, swelling, stiffness, reduced range of movement, or symptoms that may be linked to arthritis, inflammation, gout, autoimmune disease, or other musculoskeletal conditions. A focused specialist assessment can help clarify whether the problem is inflammatory, mechanical, or related to a longer-term condition.

Common symptoms

Pain in one or more joints

Pain may affect a single joint or several joints, depending on the underlying cause.

Morning stiffness

Stiffness after waking may suggest inflammatory or degenerative joint disease.

Swelling, redness, or warmth

Some causes of joint pain lead to visible swelling or inflammation in the affected joint.

Reduced movement or daily function

Symptoms may affect walking, gripping, climbing stairs, or completing normal daily tasks.

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosis begins with a careful review of symptoms and physical examination. The pattern, duration, severity, and distribution of symptoms often help distinguish between inflammatory and non-inflammatory causes of joint pain. Previous test results and further investigations may also be considered where needed.

Treatment approach

Treatment depends on the cause. It may include pain relief, control of inflammation, activity guidance, symptom monitoring, and longer-term care plans for chronic conditions. The overall aim is to improve comfort, preserve movement, and support better daily function.

When to book

Book an appointment if your joint pain lasts beyond a short period, keeps returning, is associated with swelling or stiffness, affects more than one joint, or is beginning to interfere with your quality of life and daily activity.
