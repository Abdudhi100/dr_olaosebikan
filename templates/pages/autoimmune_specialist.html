What are autoimmune diseases?

Autoimmune diseases develop when the immune system mistakenly attacks the body’s own tissues. These conditions may affect the joints, skin, muscles, blood vessels, or internal organs, and symptoms can vary widely from one person to another. Because autoimmune conditions may overlap in presentation, careful specialist evaluation is often important.

Common symptoms

Persistent fatigue

Ongoing tiredness or reduced energy can be part of autoimmune disease activity.

Joint pain and swelling

Inflammation may affect the joints and lead to pain, swelling, and stiffness.

Muscle aches

Some people experience widespread muscle discomfort or soreness alongside other symptoms.

Unexplained rashes or multi-system symptoms

Autoimmune diseases may affect the skin and other body systems at the same time.

How diagnosis is made

Diagnosis often involves a combination of medical history, symptom pattern, physical examination, and review of relevant tests. Because autoimmune diseases can mimic other illnesses, specialist assessment helps narrow the diagnosis and identify the most likely cause of ongoing symptoms.

Treatment approach

Management focuses on controlling inflammation, reducing flare-ups, monitoring organ involvement where necessary, and improving quality of life. Treatment plans are tailored to the type of disease, disease activity, symptom burden, and the patient’s individual health needs.

When to book

Book an appointment if you have symptoms suggestive of autoimmune disease, recurrent unexplained inflammation, joint pain with fatigue or rash, or an existing diagnosis that needs ongoing specialist review and long-term management.
